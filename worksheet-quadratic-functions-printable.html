    Worksheet A.4.4 Quadratic Functions
    

    Definition A.4.32. The Standard Form of a Quadratic.

    The standard form of a quadratic function is
    \begin{equation*} f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c. \end{equation*}
    🔗
    🔗

    Theorem A.4.33. The Vertex Form and Related Information.

    Let \(f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c\) be a quadratic function in standard form. The vertex form of a quadratic function is given by
    \begin{equation*} f(x) = a(x - h)^2 + k, \end{equation*}
    where \(h\) and \(k\) are the coordinates for the vertex of the function.
    🔗
    There are two important definitions related to the vertex form.
    🔗
    • The vertex of the parabola is the absolute minimum (when \(a > 0\)) or the absolute maximum (when \(a < 0\)) of the function. Note that the vertex is both a local and absolute extremum.
      🔗
      🔗
    • The axis of symmetry of a parabola is the vertical line that divides the parabola into two mirror images. Every point on one side of this line has a corresponding point on the other side that is the same distance from the line.
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    1. Using Completing the Square to Convert from Standard to Vertex Form.

    Convert \(f(x) = 3x^2 - 12x + 13\) to vertex form.
    🔗
    🔗

    Theorem A.4.35. Formula for the Vertex of a Parabola.

    Let \(f(x)=ax^2+bx+c\) be a quadratic function. Then the formula for the vertex is
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} (h,k)=\left(-\frac{b}{2a},f\left(-\frac{b}{2a}\right)\right) \end{equation*}
    🔗
    We can also rewrite this as the pair of equations
    🔗
    \begin{align*} h\amp=-\frac{b}{2a} \amp k\amp=f\left(\frac{-b}{2a}\right) \end{align*}
    🔗
    🔗

    2. Using the Formula for \(h\) and \(k\) to find the Vertex Form of a Quadratic Function.

    Write \(f(x)=2x^2-4x-6\) in vertex form.
    🔗
    🔗

    Procedure A.4.36. Graphing Quadratic Functions.

    Let \(f(x) = a(x - h)^2 + k\) be a quadratic function in vertex form. Perform the following steps to plot its graph.
    🔗
    1. Plot the vertex \((h, k)\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    2. To find another point, add \(1\) to \(h\) and add \(a \cdot 1^2\) to \(k\text{.}\) Then plot the point.
      🔗
      🔗
    3. To find an additional point, add \(2\) to \(h\) and add \(a \cdot 2^2\) to \(k\text{.}\) Then plot the point.
      🔗
      🔗
    4. Use the axis of symmetry to find and plot the last two points.
      🔗
      🔗
    5. Draw a parabola through all five points.
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    3. How to Graph a Quadratic Function, \(a > 0\).

    Graph the function \(f(x) = 2x^2 - 4x + 5\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    4. How to Graph a Quadratic Function, \(a < 0\).

    Graph the function \(f(x) = -3x^2 - 12x - 8\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    5. How to Graph a Quadratic Function Using Intercepts.

    Graph the function \(f(x) = -2x^2 - 8x - 6.\)
    🔗
    🔗
    Optimization is an area of mathematics specializing in finding the absolute extrema of an equation to solve a real-world problem. Quadratic functions give a solid introduction to the topic with many mathematical and real-world applications.
    🔗

    6. Minimizing the Distance from a Point to a Parabola.

    Find the minimum distance between the parabola \(y = x^2\) and the point \((0, 3)\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    Theorem A.4.38. Height of Projectiles.

    The height of a projectile (in feet, ignoring air resistance) is given by the equation
    \begin{equation*} h(t) = -16t^2 + v_0t + h_0, \end{equation*}
    where \(v_0\) is the initial velocity (in feet per second) and \(h_0\) is the initial height (in feet).
    🔗
    🔗

    7. Finding the Maximum Height of a Projectile.

    A soccer ball is kicked upward with an initial velocity of \(40\) ft/s from a height of \(3\) feet. Its height after \(t\) seconds is given by \(h(t) = -16t^2 + 40t + 3.\) Find the time when the ball reaches its maximum height and determine that height.
    🔗
    🔗

    8. Maximizing Revenue in Game Design.

    A mobile game sells for \(\$1.00\) and attracts \(50{,}000\) downloads. For every \(\$0.10\) increase in price, downloads decrease by \(2{,}000\text{.}\) Find the maximum revenue, the price that maximizes revenue, and the number of downloads at that price.
