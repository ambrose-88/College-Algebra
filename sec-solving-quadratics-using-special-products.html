    Subsection 4.2.1 Difference of Squares

    Theorem 4.2.1. Difference of Squares.

    For all positive real numbers \(a\text{,}\) \(b\text{,}\) the equation
    \begin{equation*} a^2 - b^2 = (a - b)(a + b) \end{equation*}
    is always true.
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 4.2.2. Solving a Difference of Squares Equation.

    Solve the equation \(36x^2 - 16 = 0\text{.}\)
    🔗
    Answer.
    \(x = \pm \frac{2}{3}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    Solution.
    The left-hand side is a difference of squares problem with \(a = 6x\) and \(b = 4\text{.}\) This implies that
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} 36x^2 - 16 = (6x - 4)(6x + 4) \end{equation*}
    🔗
    The following calculation shows the complete calculation.
    🔗
    \begin{align*} 36x^2 - 16 \amp= 0\\ (6x - 4)(6x + 4) \amp= 0\\ 6x - 4 \amp= 0 \amp 6x + 4 \amp= 0\\ 6x \amp= 4 \amp 6x \amp= -4\\ x \amp= \frac{4}{6} \amp x \amp= -\frac{4}{6}\\ x \amp= \frac{2}{3} \amp x \amp= -\frac{2}{3} \end{align*}
    🔗
    Therefore, the solutions are \(x = \pm \frac{2}{3}\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Subsection 4.2.2 Sum and Difference of Cubes

    Theorem 4.2.3. Sum of Cubes.

    For all real numbers \(a\) and \(b\text{,}\) the equation
    \begin{equation*} a^3 + b^3 = (a + b)(a^2 - ab + b^2) \end{equation*}
    is true.
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 4.2.4. Solve a Sum of Cubes Equation.

    Solve \(8x^3 + 27 = 0\text{.}\)
    🔗
    Answer.
    \(x = -\frac{3}{2}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    Solution.
    We recognize this as a sum of cubes form with \(a = 2x\) and \(b = 3\text{.}\) The following equation shows us how to find the real solutions of this equation.
    🔗
    \begin{align*} 8x^3 + 27 \amp= 0\\ (2x + 3)(4x^2 - 6x + 9) \amp= 0\\ 2x + 3 \amp= 0 \amp 4x^2 - 6x + 9 \amp= 0\\ 2x \amp= -3 \amp \text{no real solution}\\ x \amp= -\frac{3}{2} \end{align*}
    🔗
    Therefore, the only real solution is \(x = -\frac{3}{2}\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Theorem 4.2.5. Difference of Cubes.

    For all real numbers \(a\) and \(b\text{,}\) the equation
    \begin{equation*} a^3 - b^3 = (a - b)(a^2 + ab + b^2) \end{equation*}
    is true.
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 4.2.6. Solve a Difference of Cubes Equation.

    Solve \(125 - 64x^3 = 0\text{.}\)
    🔗
    Answer.
    \(x = \frac{5}{4}\)
    🔗
    🔗
    Solution.
    Solve \(125 - 64x^3 = 0\text{.}\)
    🔗
    We recognize this as a difference of cubes form with \(a = 5\) and \(b = 4x\text{.}\) The following equation shows us how to find the real solutions of this equation.
    🔗
    \begin{align*} 125 - 64x^3 \amp= 0\\ (5 - 4x)(25 + 20x + 16x^2) \amp= 0\\ 5 - 4x \amp= 0 \amp 25 + 20x + 16x^2 \amp= 0\\ -4x \amp= -5 \amp \text{no real solution}\\ x \amp= \frac{5}{4} \end{align*}
    🔗
    Therefore, the only real solution is \(x = \frac{5}{4}\text{.}\)
